How to Study in Canada from Nepal in 2026: A Step-by-Step Guide

April 10, 2026

Introduction to Studying in Canada

Canada remains one of the most sought-after destinations for students from Nepal. With its high-quality education system, multicultural environment, and excellent post-study work opportunities, it's no wonder that thousands of Nepalese students choose Canada every year.

1. Choose Your Course and Institution

The first step is to research and select a course that aligns with your career goals and academic background. Ensure that you choose a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada.

2. English Proficiency Test

Most Canadian institutions require an English proficiency test like IELTS or PTE. For the SDS stream, you generally need an IELTS score of 6.0 in each band or a PTE score of 60.

3. Financial Planning (GIC)

A Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) is a mandatory requirement for the SDS stream. Currently, the GIC amount for international students is CAD 20,635.

4. Apply for Admission

Once you've chosen your institution and met the requirements, submit your application for admission. You'll receive a Letter of Acceptance (LOA) if successful.

5. Visa Application

Submit your student visa application through the SDS or Non-SDS stream. Ensure all your documents, including your SOP and financial evidence, are in order.
